My Roblox Studio Experience

Part of where my coding journey started was with Roblox Studio. Its the coding application for Roblox, a popular gaming site that features multiple games in one service, each game being made by a different person, who uses Roblox Studio to code and publish their game. I started coding in Studio because I wanted to learn code and try to upload my own game. The above image is one of my first projects, where I combined everything I was learning to one game. I was hoping to make and release a game publicly on Roblox, but my interests are shifting, and I'm not as interested in making my career on Roblox as I used to be. However, I still intend to release a game, just on a different gaming platform, and Roblox is more like a training grounds for me.

The above image is where you download Roblox. After installing it, you can create a new project, and from there, you can do whatever you want. You can code, you can build, you can create custom meshes in a 3D modeling site like Blender and insert those into Studio, or you can do all of the above. I've done all these things in Studio, but I've had trouble with it before. It's not just getting what I want to look or work right, but it's also the flawed moderation system Roblox has that I've struggled with. I've uploaded things that were innocent and met the terms of service, but there have been several times where I was falsely flagged by the moderation system for something that was deemed "inappropriate" by the AI system. There was even a time were I was wrongly banned for 3 days from the Roblox platform for uploading action button images for my test world, and the AI thought it was inappropriate when it actually wasn't.
