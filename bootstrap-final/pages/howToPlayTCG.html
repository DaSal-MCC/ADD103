Preparation

To play the actual game, you need to form a deck of 60 cards of your choice. You are only allowed to have up to four of the same card, unless stated otherwise within the card's rules (for example, you can only have one ACE SPEC card in your deck.) The only other exception is for basic energy cards, where you can have as many as you want. You need at least one basic Pokemon in your deck - basic cards are playable right away. Without any playable Pokemon, you can't play the game.

Setup

The diagram to the right shows what your setup should look like, both yours and your opponents. Place your deck to the right. Leave space below your deck for a pile known as your discard pile. Draw 6 cards from your deck and place them to your left, face down. There are a few ways to arrange them, but one way is to spread them out in a 2x3 pattern. These are your prize cards. Draw 7 more cards from your deck into your hand, and place any basic Pokemon you find to your front, into your active spot. If there aren't any basic Pokemon in your hand, you'll have to do a mulligan, where you place those 7 cards back into your deck, shuffle your deck, and redraw seven cards, and keep repeating until you have at least one basic Pokemon drawn. If you have any extra basic Pokemon, you can place up to three of them below the Pokemon in the active spot, into an area called the benched spot. Keep your other cards in your hand. Once both players have a Pokemon in their active spot, the game can begin. Flip a coin, and call heads or tails. Whoever gets the call right, decides whether they can go first or if their opponent can go first. Whoever goes first, can't attack on their first turn.

Playing the Game

On your turn, you must draw a card from your deck. After that, you can decide to attack, or skip your turn. Before then, you can attach an energy card to a Pokemon, use trainer cards, use a Pokemon's ability, or evolve a Pokemon.

  • When you attach an energy card, you can attach it to any Pokemon you like. You can only attach one energy card to one Pokemon every turn, unless you use a Pokemon's ability that lets you attach more than one energy card. Attaching energy is important, because when you attack, you must have the correct amount of energy attached to the Pokemon, as indicated by the energy markers to the left of it's attack name. The energy stays attached unless the attack states to discard any energy.
  • You can use as many item and tool trainer cards as you like. When attaching tool cards, you can only attach one tool to each Pokemon, and it stays attached. You can only use supporter and stadium cards once per turn.
  • You can only use a Pokemon's ability if it's in the active or benched spot. You can use multiple abilities, but each ability can only be used once.
  • To evolve a Pokemon, you need the correct stage card. To evolve a basic card, place it's Stage 1 card on top of it (make sure it's the correct evolved Pokemon as indicated near the evolution marker at the top). To evolve a Stage 1 Pokemon, place the Stage 2 card on top of it. Sometimes, you can evolve a card into a special form, like it's ex form. The rules regarding evolution is that you can only evolve one Pokemon once per turn, and you can't evolve a Pokemon on your first turn.

Attacking

When attacking, make sure you have the correct amount of energy attached, as explained above. Deal the amount of damage that is indicated on the attack, listed to the right of the attack's name. Put damage indicators - circular cardboard pieces with numbers on them - on top of your opponent's Pokemon, totaling the damage amount from the attack. Most Pokemon have weaknesses or resistances - indicated at the bottom of the card - that amplify the damage dealt. Add or remove extra damage indicators when applicable, although some attacks/abilities may change the damage dealt. The damage amount from the indicators is greater than the Pokemon's health, that Pokemon is knocked out. Place it in the discard pile.

If you discard any cards, or have any Pokemon that run out of health, place them in your discard pile face-up. Your discard pile is below your deck. When you attack or skip your turn, your turn ends. Your opponent attacks next, and the game goes until someone wins.

How to Win

To win the game, you must:

  • Claim all your prize cards. To claim a prize card, you must knock out your opponent's Pokemon. Some Pokemon, when knocked out, have a special rule box that says your opponent can draw more than one prize card (usually two or three).
  • Knock out all your opponent's playable Pokemon, or put them in a situation where they can't play any Pokemon. If your opponent can't play any more Pokemon, you win the game, regardless of how many prize cards you have.
  • Empty your opponents deck (cause them to run out of cards they can draw). Each player must draw a card from their deck every turn, and if they can't, they lose.
