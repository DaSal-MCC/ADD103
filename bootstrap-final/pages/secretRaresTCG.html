How to play TCG: Basic Rules

Secret rares are special cards that have a unique appearance and art style. Their label at the bottom (000/000) is usually higher than the amount of cards that exist in a series (for example, 160/125.) Secret rares can consist of:

Full art cards are a unique card that shows the entire appearance of the Pokemon/item/supporter, spanning the entire card and outside the area of the smaller box it usually appears in. It usually has a unique front-texture, and a special holo-foil design that appears when you shine light on the card in a specific way. The text for the card's stats are printed on top of the artwork.

Rainbow rares are a more colorful version of a card. The artwork and appearance of the card is still the same, except the entire card is a rainbow-gradient color and sparkly, and some visual details from the original are left out.

Gold rares are one of the rarest cards to exist. They are not made from actual gold; however some older cards are made of real plated gold. Modern gold cards are made with foil that looks like gold, but isn't. Gold cards are typically in the hyper-rarity (see chart below), and the appearance of these cards are similar to alternate and full art cards.

Alternate art cards are similar to full art cards, in that they feature an artwork of a Pokemon/item/supporter that spans the entire card. The major difference, however, is that the artwork features the subject in some kind of unique setting and pose, and the subject itself doesn't cover the entire card. Like full art cards, the text for the card's stats are printed on top of the artwork, and the subject itself still appears in the middle-top area, similar to where it appears on basic cards.
