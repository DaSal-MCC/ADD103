How to play TCG: Basic Rules

Certain cards have a special rule box associated with them. These cards also have a special logo to indicate they're a more powerful card (ex, EX, mega EX, Mega ex, V, VStar, VMax, break, GX, V-Union.) Only certain cards are allowed in the main play of the game, also refered to as "standard play." At the time of writing this, the only advanced card types allowed in standard play are "ex" and "Mega ex". Every other card is only allowed in an alternate form of play known as "extended play."

  • EX cards are one of the first special cards to exist. They are generally more powerful than other cards, and their special rule box is that your opponent takes two prize cards if it's defeated (see "how to play" for more information on prize cards.)
  • ex cards are similar to EX cards, except they are noticably stronger though aspects like their HP, which is usually higher that 300. Their special rule box is, again, that your opponent takes two prize cards if it's defeated.
  • Mega EX cards are mega-evolved Pokemon. They are usually stronger than EX pokemon and have powerful attacks. They evolve directly from EX cards, and evolving them immediately ends your turn. Their special rule box is that your opponent takes two prize cards if it's defeated.
  • Mega ex cards are, again, mega-evolved Pokemon. They are very different, though, in that they can have just as good or even better stats than most other Pokemon; however, the drawback is that their rule box usually states that your opponent takes three prize cards if it's defeated.
  • V cards are a special kind of Pokemon card. Any pokemon can evolve from V to either VStar or VMax, if either or both variants exist for them. For instance, a V Machamp can only evolve into a VMax Machamp, because there is no VStar version of MaChamp. However, V Charizard can evolve into both VStar and VMax Charizard, since both versions exist. Their special rule box is that your opponent takes two prize cards if it's defeated.
  • VMax cards are very powerful cards, similar to ex cards. They're also similar to Mega ex cards in that their rule box also states that your opponent takes three prize cards if it's defeated.
  • VStar cards are less strong than VMax cards. To make up for that, however, they have a special "V-Star Power." It can be either an attack or an ability. Once it's used, though, it can't be used again for the rest of a game, nor can you use other VStar powers. Their special rule box is that your opponent takes two prize cards if it's defeated.
  • Break cards are a special evolution card. It's artwork shows a golden version of the evolved Pokemon, and the card is supposed to be sideways. When you use it to evolve a Pokemon, you put it on top of the card's artwork, and any attacks it has stacks with the original Pokemon's attacks. It has no special rule box.
  • GX cards are similar to VStar cards in that they also have a GX power that can only be used once in a game. Their special rule box is that your opponent takes two prize cards if it's defeated.
  • V-union cards are a more interesting but complicated version of a V card. There are four normal-sized cards that must be placed together to form one big card. There is an indicator to show that they're apart of the same card. To play them, they all must be placed in the discard pile. After that, they can be redrawn and played in battle. V-Union cards have multiple attacks and abilities, and have very high HP. Their special rule box is that your opponent takes three prize cards if it's defeated.
